## Transcript

### Question 1
Describe the situation in which you would use a digital version of Blokus.

**Answer**
I’d use a digital version of Blokus during a casual game night with my spouse and teenage kids, when we’re all home and looking for something quick but still mentally engaging. The physical game is fun, but setting up the board and pieces takes time, and sometimes someone might not be in the mood for a long setup. A digital version would let us jump right in—no waiting for pieces to be arranged, no worrying about losing a tile. Since the rules are simple (“place your pieces so they touch only at corners, and try to block others”), the digital version could even offer a one-minute tutorial or a quick recap, which would make it easy for my younger kids to pick up. We’d play a few rounds, maybe switch up the difficulty or try a solo mode against an AI if someone wants a challenge. It’s perfect for a short evening slot—maybe 30 minutes to an hour—without feeling rushed. The replayability comes from the variety of strategies and the fact that even with the same rules, each game feels different depending on who’s playing and how the pieces fall. Plus, since it’s digital, we could even play remotely if someone’s out of town, which would work well with our mixed-skill household.

### Question 2
What main value would you expect from such a game?

**Answer**
The main value would be the combination of **ease of access** and **mental engagement without hassle**. A digital version removes the friction of physical setup—no scrambling for tiles, no worrying about losing pieces—and lets us dive straight into the fun. Since the rules are simple but still strategic, it’s a great way to keep everyone, from my 12-year-old to my spouse, involved without anyone feeling left out. The replayability is a bonus because even though the core rules stay the same, the way pieces fit together and the tactics shift each time, so no two games feel identical. Plus, being able to play remotely if someone’s away adds flexibility to our game nights. It’s not just about saving time—it’s about making sure the game feels fresh, fair, and fun for everyone, no matter their skill level.
