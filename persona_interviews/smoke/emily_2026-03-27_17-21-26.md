# Interview Transcript: Emily

## Persona

- **Name:** Emily
- **Age:** 34
- **Technical experience:** moderate
- **Goals:** play a strategy game that is easy to teach to friends and family, fit a full game into a short evening slot, find a game with high replayability and low setup effort
- **Constraints:** mixed-skill household, limited time for game nights, prefers rules that can be explained in under a minute

## Questionnaire

1. Describe the situation in which you would use a digital version of Blokus.
2. What main value would you expect from such a game?

## Transcript

### Question 1
Describe the situation in which you would use a digital version of Blokus.

**Answer**
It’s usually a Friday or Saturday evening after work. I try to get a few friends over, and sometimes I’ll invite my nieces and nephews to keep things interesting since we have mixed skills in the house. We only have a couple of hours max before people need to wrap up, so I really need to minimize the time we spend just getting the board set up or trying to explain complex rules. If the digital version handles the setup and the tutorial, that gives us more actual game time, which is what everyone is there for.

### Question 2
What main value would you expect from such a game?

**Answer**
I’d say the main value is **convenience and low friction**. I want to sit down, tap a button or pick up a controller, and get right into the action without dealing with manual setup or rulebooks. If I have to spend ten minutes explaining complex mechanics to my friends or setting up physical tiles, it kills the vibe. I value a game that respects my time and lets the strategy and the social interaction start almost instantly.
